What is the Bhagavad Gita?

The Bhagavad Gita ("Song of God") is a talk between a worried warrior named Arjuna and his wise friend Krishna, right before a big war. Arjuna is too scared and confused to fight. Krishna helps him — and that talk turns into a simple guide on how to live, how to handle hard times, and how to find peace.

🌌

Why it matters today

Its themes — anxiety, duty, decision-making under pressure, finding meaning — are exactly the struggles of modern life.

🧭

A practical guide

Not abstract theory. The Gita offers a usable framework for work, relationships, stress and self-mastery.

🕊️

Open to everyone

Students, professionals, families, seekers and the simply curious — no prior knowledge required to begin.

Learning Path

How to journey through the Gita

Start with the despair (Ch. 1)

Meet Arjuna's crisis — the human moment everyone recognises.

Learn the core teaching (Ch. 2–6)

The soul, selfless action, meditation and the steady mind.

Deepen with devotion & vision (Ch. 7–12)

The divine nature, the cosmic form, and the path of love.

Refine character & liberation (Ch. 13–18)

The gunas, virtue, faith, and the great synthesis of surrender.

Knowledge Map

How the core ideas connect

The Gita's concepts form a web. Action leads to discipline, discipline to mastery, mastery to detachment, and detachment back to peaceful action.

Desire → Destruction (Ch. 2.62–63)

Dwelling on objects breeds attachment → attachment breeds desire → desire breeds anger → anger breeds delusion → delusion destroys reason. The Gita's most famous chain of cause and effect.

The path upward

Selfless action → discipline → a steady mind → wisdom → detachment → freedom. Each rung supports the next.
